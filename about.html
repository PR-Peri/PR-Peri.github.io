I find it amazing to watch as the digital revolution sculpts society at a rate that has never before been seen, there is so much to still be discovered. Big data industry is a topic that particularly interests me, stemming from my studies and keen interest in statistics. I would like to explore on how these interfaces work, the theory behind computers and its methodology.

I completed my Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science, with a major of Data Science at Multimedia University (MMU), Malaysia. To me, data science is an interdisciplinary field that consists of math, statistics, software, and algorithmic thinking. On top of those, depending on what we work on, domain knowledge can be a significant requirement. Thus, I accepted there is too much to learn and it’d be an unnecessary burden to even try.

During my university days, I spent most of my time joining hackathons to understand what is truly on demand and which path I should focus on more. So, I took a lot of interesting courses like Visual Information processing, Data Mining, Digital Forensics, Computer Architecture, and many more subjects to completely immerse myself in big data and machine learning.
